## What exists now that didn't 90 days ago

The MRM function at Tessera now covers
AI/ML. Specifically:

- An updated MRM Policy (v4.3) with a
  decision-influence-based definition of
  "model" that catches AI/ML systems.
- A complete 42-model AI/ML inventory.
- A 3-tier tiering framework, applied.
- An SR 11-7-adapted validation methodology
  documented and exam-ready.
- A six-stream ongoing monitoring framework
  in phased operation.
- The CAO × MRM × Audit split-authorship
  pattern as the working operating model.

## What got built vs. plan

Achieved on plan: policy, inventory,
tiering, methodology, monitoring framework
documentation, Stream 1/2/5/6 operational
coverage for Tier-1 and Tier-2.

Behind plan: validation completion (6 of
28 new-to-MRM models validated vs. planned
8); Stream-3 monitoring buildout for
non-Tier-1 models (deferred to Q1 per
phased capability buildout).

Ahead of plan: the Treasury finding
response (operational pause and
provisional validation within 14 days of
discovery vs. the slower-default for a
governance gap).

## What we got wrong

Three substantive items, per honesty
discipline.

**1. We underestimated the Treasury
finding's blast radius on the 90-day arc.**
The Treasury "smart cash position"
forecasting tool's discovery on Day 22
consumed 4 validator-weeks of capacity
that the original plan had budgeted for
other tier-1 validations. The Tessera-
right answer was clearly to prioritise
Treasury; the planning error was not
budgeting for discovery findings of this
size. Future inventory work should plan a
discovery buffer.

**2. The wealth-management retrain
investigation took longer than it should
have.** The systemic cause (change-
management gate not operating after
training data refresh) was identifiable
within 3 days; we took 9 days, in part
because the investigation was led by
business-line analytics with insufficient
MRM-side involvement. Going forward,
mod-110 systemic-cause investigations
should be led by MRM with business-line
support, not the reverse.

**3. We under-communicated to Internal
Audit during the 90-day arc.** Audit
learned about the framework details
through the Q4 Board package preparation
in Week 11 rather than through earlier
engagement. Audit is now playing catch-up
ahead of Q1 audit scope-setting. Going
forward, monthly Audit engagement is the
right cadence during framework buildout
phases.

## What's fragile

Three specific fragilities, not generic
concerns.

**1. MRM validator capacity through Q1.**
Without the +2 validator hires, the Q1
validation pipeline will slip. The CFO
decision is in flight; risk: hiring
takes 4-5 months in this market; even
approval-today produces validators
landing in Q2. Q1 will be tight.

**2. The Stream-3 monitoring buildout
sequencing.** Stream-3 (performance vs.
ground truth) for non-Tier-1 models is
deferred to Q1. If a Tier-2 model has a
performance issue in Q4 that Stream-3
would have caught, we will not catch it
until Q1 when Stream-3 is operational.
The compensating control is Stream-1
(input drift) and Stream-2 (output
drift), which catch some but not all
Stream-3-class failure modes.

**3. The HR vendor LLM validation under
information asymmetry.** Vendor has so
far declined to provide model card
detail beyond what's in the standard
sales documentation. The validation
under information asymmetry will be
explicit about what cannot be validated.
If the EEOC or a state agency presses on
subgroup outcomes, Tessera's answer
("vendor declined to provide; we
monitor outcomes ourselves") is honest
but defensive.

## The CAO × MRM × Audit triangle

The split-authorship boundary held up under
90 days of real work, with one notable
strain point and one notable strength.

**Strain point.** When the Treasury finding
surfaced, the question of whether CAO or
MRM led the response was genuinely
ambiguous. The CAO surfaced it; MRM was
the validator-of-record; the CRO was the
ultimate accountable. We resolved it by
joint CAO+MRM ownership for the
investigation with CRO as decision-maker.
This worked but only because the CAO and
CRO have a productive working relationship.
A version of this triangle with personality
friction would have produced delay.

**Strength.** The validation methodology
authorship (CAO) and validation execution
(MRM) split worked cleanly. MRM validators
have engaged the methodology substantively;
when their experience with execution
suggests methodology refinements, they
surface them and the CAO updates the
methodology with their input. The
collaboration shape — joint authorship
under CAO ownership — has held.

**Audit observation.** Internal Audit needs
more visibility into the framework
buildout. The under-communication noted
above was not strategic but accidental; the
Q1 audit work will benefit from monthly
Audit engagement going forward.

## Reasoning notes

- **Why I named the Audit under-
  communication.** Honesty discipline.
  Audit will notice it during Q1 work; the
  CAO naming it first preserves the
  working relationship.
- **Why the Q1 plan named specific dates
  rather than ranges.** The Risk
  Committee, the CRO, and the CFO all
  need to act on these dates. Vague ranges
  produce planning failures.
- **Why the boundary triangle section is
  detailed.** mod-104 §7 boundary strain
  pattern was the explicit prediction.
  The synthesis is the right place to
  evaluate whether the prediction held.
- **What I would do differently.** I might
  have set up the Audit engagement cadence
  earlier in the 90-day arc. Audit
  buy-in to a framework before exam
  pressure is qualitatively different
  from buy-in under exam-prep pressure.
