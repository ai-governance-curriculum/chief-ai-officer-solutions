# Deliverable 03 — Year-1 Operating Plan — Reference Solution

## 1. Solution overview

Year-1 operating plan for Northrise. Centralised-
with-hub-and-spoke operating model (per
Deliverable 01). Quarterly milestones distributed
across Q1-Q4 with substantial Q1 work already
committed (the 7 capstone deliverables). 5-role
hiring sequence within the $4M budget envelope.
Explicit defer list includes: comprehensive
automation, full EU AI Act conformity
documentation, custom trust architecture build.
Mid-year-1 inflection point handled via
deliberate Board chair pre-briefings. Year-1
success criteria explicitly stated.

---

## 2. The plan

### Operating model (confirmed)

Per Deliverable 01: centralised CAO function
with hub-and-spoke execution. Hub: CAO function
of 5 roles. Spokes: product-line representatives
in the AI Review Board.

### Quarterly milestones

**Q1 (covered by the 90-day capstone arc):**

- Day 10: Program charter (Deliverable 01).
- Day 18: AI risk taxonomy (Deliverable 02).
- Day 25: Year-1 operating plan (this
  deliverable, Deliverable 03).
- Day 45: AI risk appetite statement
  (Deliverable 04).
- Day 60: Trust architecture decision
  (Deliverable 05).
- Day 75: Compliance operations baseline
  (Deliverable 06).
- Day 90: First quarterly Board report
  (Deliverable 07).
- AI Risk Council operational from week 4; AI
  Review Board operational from week 6.

**Q2:**

- AI vendor risk assessment overlay built
  (covers foundation-model providers + AI
  infrastructure vendors).
- Customer-deployed-risk control guidance for
  Agent Platform customers (the bridge between
  Northrise's appetite and customer-side
  configuration).
- ISO 42001 readiness assessment commissioned
  (external consultant).
- First substantive tabletop exercise.

**Q3:**

- Trust architecture deployment (per
  Deliverable 05 decision) operational.
- Audit ledger / evidence layer operational at
  baseline level.
- Bias monitoring on Analytics product (the
  most material bias surface).
- First AI Risk Council strategic review.

**Q4:**

- Year-1 self-assessment completed.
- Q2-Q4 standards drafted (currently year 1
  produces only load-bearing standards).
- Cross-product taxonomy uniformity validated.
- Year-2 plan drafted and Board-ready.

### Hiring sequence

| Hire | Quarter | Role | Background priority |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Q1 (already underway) | AI Risk Lead | Risk professional with SaaS / startup experience |
| 2 | Q1 | Policy and Standards Lead | Compliance background; AI familiarity |
| 3 | Q2 | Evidence and Operations Lead | Engineering or audit background |
| 4 | Q2-Q3 | Customer-Deployed Risk Specialist | Customer success or technical account management background; Northrise-specific role |
| 5 | Q3-Q4 | Regulatory Engagement Lead | Legal or compliance background; EU AI Act familiarity |

The fourth hire (Customer-Deployed Risk
Specialist) is Northrise-specific. Most
Halverston / Tessera reference solutions don't
have this role because their products don't have
customer-edge deployment.

### What year 1 will not do

Explicitly deferred to year 2 or later:

- **Comprehensive compliance automation.** Per
  mod-109 §4.3 trap. Year 1 builds practice;
  year 2 considers automation.
- **Full EU AI Act conformity documentation
  for all high-risk customer deployments.**
  Year 1 establishes the framework and
  customer guidance; full per-deployment
  documentation is year 2 work.
- **Custom trust architecture build.** Per
  Deliverable 05, year 1 buys/partners; custom
  build (if needed) is year 3+.
- **Comprehensive customer-side bias
  monitoring.** Year 1 establishes
  Northrise-side monitoring on Analytics; full
  customer-side monitoring for Agent Platform
  is year 2-3 work.
- **Multiple tabletop exercises.** One in year
  1; more is performance art at year-1
  maturity.

### Mid-year-1 inflection point preparation

Around months 6-8, foundation work will feel
invisible. Preparation:

- Q3 quarterly report's material-changes
  section explicitly shows foundation
  progress.
- Pre-briefings with the CEO and Audit
  Committee chair in late Q2 frame what Q3
  will look like.
- A specific monthly check-in with the CEO
  during Q3 to surface the perceived
  invisibility before it becomes a CEO concern.

The Series D fundraising activity in Q3 will
amplify the invisibility pressure; the
preparation specifically anticipates this.

### Year-1 success criteria

Year 1 succeeded if:

1. AI Risk Council and AI Review Board operate
   with documented decisions and meeting
   minutes.
2. Risk appetite statement adopted by Board.
3. All in-scope AI capabilities inventoried with
   impact assessments.
4. Bias monitoring operational on Analytics;
   customer-deployment guidance available for
   Agent Platform.
5. ISO 42001 readiness assessment complete with
   gap analysis.
6. First five hires in role.
7. Quarterly Board reporting operational.
8. CAO × CTO and CAO × Head of Security
   boundaries documented.

### Budget posture

Year-1 budget framing for $4M:

- **$1.6M CAO and team compensation** (CAO +
  5 hires fully loaded).
- **$1.2M vendor / tooling.** Audit ledger
  partner; trust architecture vendor (per
  Deliverable 05); ISO 42001 consultant; one
  tabletop facilitator.
- **$0.5M legal and regulatory engagement.**
  EU AI Act counsel; SOC 2 / ISO 42001 audit
  costs.
- **$0.7M contingency and program operations.**

Each line tied to risk reduction per mod-112
§6.3 discipline.

## 3. Reasoning notes

- **Why I made the Customer-Deployed Risk
  Specialist a year-1 hire.** The Agent
  Platform's risk surface is unique to
  Northrise; the role doesn't exist in
  reference patterns from other modules.
  Hiring this in year 1 reduces the lag
  between Agent Platform growth and Northrise's
  ability to govern the customer-edge surface.
- **Why the budget allocates substantially to
  vendor / tooling.** Year-1 trust architecture
  and evidence layer decisions (per
  Deliverables 05 and the next iteration's
  evidence work) likely involve commercial
  vendors. Building these in-house with
  Northrise's $4M envelope isn't feasible.
- **Why the "what year 1 will not do" list is
  substantive.** Programs that try to do
  everything in year 1 produce shallow
  results. Naming the defer list explicitly
  protects against scope creep from CEO and
  Board enthusiasm.
- **What I would have done differently.** I
  might have committed to Q2 instead of Q3 on
  the customer-deployed-risk control guidance.
  The Agent Platform's growth rate suggests
  this is more urgent than Q3 timing
  acknowledges. Trade-off was Q1 capacity
  constraints on the small team.
