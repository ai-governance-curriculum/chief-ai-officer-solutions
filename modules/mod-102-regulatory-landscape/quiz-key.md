# Module 102 — Quiz Answer Key

Answers and short explanations for the [Module 102 quiz](https://github.com/ai-infra-curriculum/ai-infra-chief-ai-officer-learning/blob/main/lessons/mod-102-regulatory-landscape/quiz.md).

---

## Section A — The four lineages (§1)

**Q1. e.** Vendor-based is not one of the four lineages. The
four named in §1 are rights-based, sector-based,
capability-based, and jurisdiction-based.

**Q2. b.** NYC Local Law 144 is sector-based — it regulates
AI specifically in the employment context. There is a
rights-based read of the law (candidate transparency), but
the regulation's organizing principle is the HR sector.

**Q3.** Any two of: EU AI Act (rights + capability +
jurisdiction); GDPR Art. 22 (rights + jurisdiction); CA AI
Transparency Act (rights + jurisdiction); NYDFS Part 500 AI
amendments (sector + jurisdiction).

---

## Section B — EU AI Act risk tiers (§2)

**Q4. d. Minimal-risk.** Minimal-risk systems carry no
specific obligations under the Act.

**Q5. c. Title III.** Annex III is the list of high-risk
areas; Annex III is referenced from Art. 6, which sits in
Title III.

**Q6. b.** Real-time remote biometric identification in
public spaces by law enforcement (with narrow exceptions)
is the prohibited practice. (a), (c), and (d) are regulated
but not prohibited.

**Q7.** Art. 6(3) is the high-risk-system *exemption*: a
system that falls within an Annex III area is NOT high-risk
if it performs a narrow procedural task, improves a
previously completed human activity, detects decision-making
patterns without replacing the prior assessment, or
performs a preparatory task. The operator must document
the assessment.

---

## Section C — EU AI Act operational provisions (§2)

**Q8. b.** Established, implemented, documented, and
maintained on a continuous, iterative basis. The "established
before launch and audited annually" framing (a) is a common
mis-read and is wrong: the RMS is continuous.

**Q9. c. Art. 72.** Post-market monitoring is the separable
named artifact concept that distinguishes EU AI Act from
NIST AI RMF.

**Q10. b. 2 days.** Fundamental-rights-violation incidents
must be reported within 2 days. (a) is for critical-
infrastructure threats; (c) is for other serious incidents.

**Q11. b.** Art. 11 + Annex IV govern the technical
documentation.

**Q12.** The function structure (GOVERN / MAP / MEASURE /
MANAGE) lines up with the Article 9 elements ((a)
identification → MAP; (b)/(d) estimation, evaluation, and
measures → MEASURE + MANAGE; the RMS itself → GOVERN), but
the EU AI Act treats post-market monitoring as a separable
named artifact while NIST treats monitoring as a continuous
activity within MEASURE.

## Section D — NIST AI RMF Playbook (§3)

**Q13. a. GOVERN-1.6.** The AI system inventory anchor.

**Q14. False.** The lecture notes recommend reading the
Playbook *selectively, question-driven* — not end-to-end.

**Q15.** Any answer naming MANAGE-3.1 (AI risks and benefits
from third-party resources are regularly monitored) with a
one-sentence justification — third-party AI is the most
common silent expansion of risk surface.

---

## Section E — Sector-specific (§4)

**Q16. b.** The CFPB's stance is that the "black-box defense"
does not work — ECOA's adverse-action-notice obligations
apply regardless of model interpretability.

**Q17. c.** An EU medical-device AI system may be subject to
both EU MDR and EU AI Act high-risk provisions. Both Acts
have coordination provisions but the practical work is
harmonising the two technical files.

**Q18.** Any answer naming a sector regulation that predates
AI-specific rules — ECOA, SR 11-7, FDA SaMD, HIPAA — with a
one-sentence note on how it is now applied to AI (e.g., SR
11-7 treating ML models as a sub-category of "model" within
its existing supervision).

---

## Section F — US state patchwork + jurisdictional discipline (§5–§6)

**Q19. c. AI system.** The lecture notes are explicit:
organize by system, not regulator.

**Q20.** Any answer naming a specific failure mode from §5
or §6 — e.g., "treating each state law as a separate
compliance project produces fragmented coverage and
state-by-state inconsistency", or "rebuilding the program
every time a new regulation appears produces regulatory
whiplash (mod-101 §6)".
