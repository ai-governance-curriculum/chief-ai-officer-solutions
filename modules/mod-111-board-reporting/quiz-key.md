# Module 111 — Quiz Answer Key

Answers and explanations for the [Module 111 quiz](https://github.com/ai-infra-curriculum/ai-infra-chief-ai-officer-learning/blob/main/lessons/mod-111-board-reporting/quiz.md).

---

## Section A — What boards need (§1)

**Q1. d. Operational latency metrics** is not one
of the four. §1.2 — boards need risk posture,
material exceptions, material decisions pending,
material changes ahead.

**Q2. False.** §1.4 + §1.3 — board reports are
shorter than operational reports because the
audience exercises oversight, not management.

**Q3.** Any answer naming oversight as evaluation
of management's competence vs. management as
running operations. Example: "oversight
determines whether management is doing its job
competently and accountably; management decides
how to allocate resources, design processes, and
run operations."

---

## Section B — Risk appetite statement (§2)

**Q4. b.** CAO authors; board adopts. §2.1.

**Q5. d. Forecast language** is not one of the
three §2.4 patterns. The three are risk-class,
boundary, threshold.

**Q6.** Any §2.5 category with reasoning.
Example: "bias and fairness — the impossibility
result from mod-105 §3.2 means appetite cannot
simultaneously cover all fairness conceptions; the
statement must name which take priority."

---

## Section C — Quarterly board reporting (§3)

**Q7. b. 3-5 pages.** §3.1.

**Q8. c. Asks.** §3.2 — most underused section.

**Q9. False.** §3.3 — honesty discipline; bad
news softened produces post-hoc embarrassment.

**Q10.** Any answer naming brief-the-chair, ad-
hoc-update, or pre-meeting briefing. Example:
"the off-cycle update discipline brings material
matters to the Board Risk Committee chair
between regular meetings rather than waiting; the
chair decides whether to convene early."

---

## Section D — Materiality (§4)

**Q11. c.** Reasonable user would make a
different decision. §4.1.

**Q12. d. Aesthetic** is not a dimension. The
five are financial, regulatory, reputational,
operational, strategic.

**Q13. c.** Escalated through CRO + Board Risk
Committee chair for disposition. §4.4 + §4.5.

**Q14.** Any §4.3 dimension with example.
Example: "financial — > 1% of annual revenue or
> $X — example: $X+ in AI-incident-related
customer remediation."

---

## Section E — The CAO at the board table (§5)

**Q15. d. Direct disagreement** is *recommended*
per §5.3, not avoided. The avoidances are
overclaiming, hedging, education-as-answer.

**Q16. b.** Say so respectfully and directly.
§5.3.

**Q17. False.** §5.4 — board substance is
confidential; informal sharing breaks the
discipline.

**Q18.** Any answer naming specific-when-known
+ honest-about-unknown. Example: "the honesty
discipline at the board table means giving
specific answers when you have them and honestly
saying 'I don't know — I will report back by
[date]' when you don't."

---

## Section F — Annual self-assessment (§6)

**Q19. c. Internal audit findings restated** —
self-assessment is separate from internal audit
per §6.1; it doesn't restate audit findings.

**Q20.** Any answer naming the peer-CAO test and
its rationale. Example: "the honesty test asks
whether the CAO would be comfortable showing the
self-assessment to a peer CAO at a similar
institution who would give an honest read; if yes,
the self-assessment is probably honest; the test
matters because the alternative is self-serving
documents that confirm the program is operating
when it might not be."
