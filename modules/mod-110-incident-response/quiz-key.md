# Module 110 — Quiz Answer Key

Answers and explanations for the [Module 110 quiz](https://github.com/ai-governance-curriculum/chief-ai-officer-learning/blob/main/lessons/mod-110-incident-response/quiz.md).

---

## Section A — What AI IR is (§1)

**Q1. d.** Resolution always takes longer is not
named; the others are §1.2 characteristics.

**Q2. b.** Replace the CISO's IR operations. §1.3 —
the CAO function does **not** do this.

**Q3.** Any §1.1 element. Example: "the five-phase
lifecycle — preparation, detection and analysis,
containment-eradication-recovery, and post-incident
activity."

---

## Section B — Detection and first hour (§2)

**Q4. d.** Author the post-incident review is not
a first-hour decision; the others are (§2.2).

**Q5. b.** Removes historical record, hides
investigation, delays response if real. §2.4.

**Q6.** Any answer naming the not-yet-confirmed
problem. Example: "in the first hour, the team
often doesn't know whether the incident is real;
the discipline is provisional classification with
explicit provisional status rather than waiting."

---

## Section C — Containment (§3)

**Q7. c.** Public statement of fault is not one of
the containment options; it's a communication
response. The others are §3.1 options.

**Q8. a. Overcontainment.** §3.3.

**Q9. False.** §3.5 — containment is provisional,
not final; the response team revisits the posture
at every working session.

**Q10.** Any answer naming the no-one-blamed-for-
caution logic. Example: "overcontainment's
political logic is that no one gets blamed for
being too cautious — but it imposes real costs
that aren't immediately visible."

---

## Section D — Notification matrix (§4)

**Q11. b. 2 days.** EU AI Act Art. 73 fundamental-
rights timeline.

**Q12. b.** At program quiet times, pre-computed
per classification. §4.3.

**Q13.** Any answer naming the hour-24 decision
point. Example: "treat hour 24 of the incident
response as the 'are we notifying or not' deadline
for most obligations; by hour 24 the team has
enough information to decide."

**Q14. False.** §4.6 — customer notification may
not precede regulatory in some regimes;
coordination matters.

---

## Section E — Investigation (§5)

**Q15. c. Has unlimited scope** is not a property
of working investigation. §5.1 — investigation
can expand, but the *initial* scope is named.

**Q16. a. NTSB-style investigation.** §5.4.

**Q17.** Any answer distinguishing proximate
(what immediately caused the outcome) from
systemic (organisational / policy / process gap
that allowed the proximate cause).

---

## Section F — Post-incident review and tabletop (§6)

**Q18. b.** Tabletop exercises in rotation across
scenario types. §6.5.

**Q19. c. Same scenario repeatedly** — §6.6
anti-pattern.

**Q20.** Any §6.1 + §6.3 combination. Example: "a
working review is conducted within 30 days of
incident closure, led by someone not in the chain
of responsibility, and produces specific recommendations
that are assigned to a specific role with a
specific timeline."
