# Module 109 — Quiz Answer Key

Answers and explanations for the [Module 109 quiz](https://github.com/ai-infra-curriculum/ai-infra-chief-ai-officer-learning/blob/main/lessons/mod-109-compliance-operations/quiz.md).

---

## Section A — What compliance operations is (§1)

**Q1. b. Compliance operations.** §1.

**Q2. c.** Continuous evidence emitted as side
effect is the *opposite* of the failure mode; it's
what working operations looks like (§1.2).

**Q3.** Any answer naming policy as what-we-say and
operations as what-we-do. Example: "policy is the
standards document; operations is the day-to-day
work of operating against the standards."

---

## Section B — Control mapping (§2)

**Q4. d. Vendor** is not one of the six. The six
are obligation, control, evidence, owner, cadence,
test.

**Q5. b.** Build one control with cross-referenced
obligations — the §2.3 crosswalk pattern.

**Q6. b. An attested AI Risk Council decision** is
the only one that *is* a control; the others (a,
c, d) are not (§2.5).

---

## Section C — Continuous evidence collection (§3)

**Q7. c. Being audited, not operated.** §3.1.

**Q8. d. Vendor cadence** is not one of the three.

**Q9.** Any answer naming the weakness-of-attestation
problem. Example: "over-using attested evidence
produces attestation fatigue — owners rubber-stamp
because they're asked too often, and the evidence
becomes weak."

**Q10. False.** §3.5 — one-time controls operate
on event, not weekly.

---

## Section D — Compliance automation (§4)

**Q11. c. Judgment-laden decisions** are §4.2 (not
worth automating).

**Q12. b. Adopting comprehensive platforms before
underlying control discipline exists** — §4.3.

**Q13.** Any §4.2 category. Example: "judgment-
laden decisions — whether an incident counts as
EU AI Act Art. 73 serious is a judgment; the
automation can pre-classify, the decision is human."

---

## Section E — ISO 42001 Annex A (§5)

**Q14. b. About 10.** §5.1.

**Q15. d. Lacks legal authority** — Annex A is
authoritative as part of a published standard.
The other three are real limitations per §5.4.

**Q16.** Any §5.2 step. Example: "map each Annex A
control to a program control; identify gaps where
no mapping exists."

---

## Section F — CAO × Compliance boundary (§6)

**Q17. c. Separate AI compliance function running
in parallel to enterprise Compliance** — §6.4
failing pattern.

**Q18. False.** §6.5 — CCO typically reports to
GC or CEO; CAO typically reports to CRO. They
are peer second-line functions.

**Q19. c. AI-specific regulator engagement** is
in the CAO's scope per §6.2, not Compliance's
per §6.1.

**Q20.** Any §6.4 working pattern. Example: "CAO
controls extend Compliance's catalog rather than
duplicating; shared evidence infrastructure;
joint quarterly obligations-register review;
CAO contributes AI expertise to Compliance-led
investigations rather than running parallel
investigations."
