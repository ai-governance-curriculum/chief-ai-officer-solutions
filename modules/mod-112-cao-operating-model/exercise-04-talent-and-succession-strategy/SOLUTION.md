# Exercise 04 — Talent and Succession Strategy — Reference Solution

## 1. Solution overview

Talent strategy for **Halverston Capital** at end
of year 2. Capability gap on AI vendor risk
specifically; mitigation is consultant-engaged
specialist plus targeted hiring in year 3.
Immediate succession to the AI Risk Lead with
60-day interim CAO period. Short-term succession
candidates: AI Risk Lead and an external pipeline
of two named individuals. The CAO's own
replaceability posture: making the function
document itself sufficiently that any qualified
successor could operate it.

---

## 2. Artifact 1 — Talent strategy

### Current talent profile

**In place (year 2 end):** 10 net new roles in
the CAO function across years 1-2. AI Risk Lead
(2 yrs in role); Policy Lead (2 yrs); Evidence
Lead (2 yrs); Regulatory Engagement Lead (1 yr);
Algorithm Validation Lead (1 yr); Trust
Architecture Lead (1 yr); plus four supporting
roles.

**Honest assessment:** The team has
substantively learned the program; the AI Risk
Lead is performing at deputy-CAO level; the
Policy Lead has matured into a senior
practitioner; the Algorithm Validation Lead is
solid; the Trust Architecture Lead is excellent.

**Gaps:** Three capability gaps remain. (1)
Vendor AI risk specialisation — the gap from
mod-109 Ex-03 and mod-112 Ex-02. (2) Board
engagement specialisation — currently the CAO
handles all board work personally, which is a
year-3 / year-4 transition needed for the CAO
to operate at year-3 level. (3) Communications
capability — the program produces written work
of varying quality; a senior communicator role
would lift quality.

### Year-3 hiring plan

Next 3 hires in year 3, in priority order:

| Hire | Q | Role | Background | Reasoning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Q1 | Deputy AI Risk Lead | Risk professional, 5+ yrs experience | Key-person risk mitigation; succession track |
| 2 | Q2 | Vendor AI Risk Specialist | Vendor risk + AI background | Closes the long-deferred third-party gap |
| 3 | Q3 | Board / Communications Lead | Senior communicator + governance experience | Lifts written quality; supports CAO at year-3 level |

Each role's profile is specific; recruiting
timelines are calibrated to market.

### Capability gaps

**Vendor AI risk specialisation** is the binding
gap. Year 1 deferred it; year 2 deferred it; year
3 must close it.

**Non-hiring mitigation:** Engage a vendor-AI-
risk consultant for year 3 specifically; they
operate in the gap while the hire (above) is
recruited. The consultant transfers knowledge to
the hire. This addresses the market constraint
on hiring (the specialist role is genuinely
hard to fill) without leaving the gap unstaffed.

Consultant cost: ~$300K for the year. Acceptable
given the gap's age and the program's exposure.

### Talent risks

1. **Key-person dependency on AI Risk Lead.**
   The AI Risk Lead's departure would
   substantially destabilise the program.
   *Mitigation:* Deputy AI Risk Lead recruitment
   in year 3 (#1 above); documented operating
   model that the deputy can operate against.
2. **Vendor AI risk specialist recruiting risk.**
   The market is tight; we may not fill the
   role on the year-3 timeline.
   *Mitigation:* Consultant engagement (above);
   extended search timeline acceptable; CRO
   informed of the risk.
3. **Compensation pressure.** AI roles are in
   demand; retention of current team requires
   compensation reviews.
   *Mitigation:* Annual market benchmarking;
   competitive but not market-leading; relying
   partly on the program's substance to
   sustain commitment.

### Development paths

The current team's growth paths:

- **AI Risk Lead → Deputy CAO** by end of year 3
  (per the succession discussion below).
- **Policy Lead → senior strategic role** with
  broader scope across enterprise compliance.
- **Algorithm Validation Lead → leadership of
  larger algorithm team** as Halverston's AI
  portfolio grows.
- **Trust Architecture Lead → senior engineering
  role** in either the CTO organisation or
  CISO organisation.

Each is named in the development plan for the
individual. The CAO meets with each direct
report quarterly to review.

## 3. Artifact 2 — Succession plan

### Immediate succession (6 months, if CAO leaves unexpectedly)

**Interim CAO:** AI Risk Lead.

**Top 3 areas requiring immediate attention:**

1. **Regulator-facing relationships.** The CAO
   has cultivated relationships with the EU AI
   Act NCA, NYDFS, and state insurance
   regulators across Halverston's footprint.
   Interim CAO must engage these within 30 days
   to maintain continuity.
2. **Board engagement.** Quarterly Board Risk
   Committee report continues; interim CAO
   prepares with Board chair pre-briefing.
3. **AI Risk Council operation.** Monthly
   meeting cadence continues; interim CAO
   chairs.

**Top 3 risks the interim must monitor:**

1. Pending third-party governance gap closure
   (now under interim CAO ownership).
2. Cross-LOB taxonomy uniformity work in
   progress.
3. Active regulator matters (one open EU AI Act
   notification matter; one CFPB inquiry).

**Documented support:** The CAO's operating
documentation (the standards, the appetite
statement, the calendar, the recurring rhythm
calendar from Exercise 03) provides the
interim CAO with operating instructions.

### Short-term succession (1-2 years)

**Internal candidates (named):**

1. **AI Risk Lead** — currently positioned as
   primary internal successor. Year-3
   development includes: more independent
   board interaction; one external speaking
   engagement quarterly; cross-functional
   leadership of the third-party gap closure.
2. **Algorithm Validation Lead** — secondary
   internal successor. Year-3 development
   includes: leading the cross-LOB taxonomy
   uniformity workshop; one regulator-facing
   moment.

**External pipeline (described, not named):**

The CAO maintains regular contact with two
external individuals at peer institutions who
could be candidates if internal succession isn't
available. Annual conversation maintained.

### Longer-term succession (3-5 years)

The CAO function will, by year 5, have:

- Multiple internal candidates ready (current
  AI Risk Lead, current Algorithm Validation
  Lead, the deputy hires from year 3, and
  one or two others).
- Documented operating model sufficient that
  any qualified successor could operate it.
- External pipeline including peer-CAOs at
  comparable institutions.

The current CAO is committing to making the
function continuously succession-ready —
documented enough that the function does not
require the current CAO to operate.

### CAO's own posture

Per §6.4, the discipline is being replaceable.

What the current CAO is doing now to be
replaceable:

- **Documenting the operating model itself**
  (per the Exercise 01 plan, the Exercise 03
  calendar, the year-on-year self-assessments).
- **Visible delegation.** Routinely deferring
  to deputies on operational decisions; the
  pattern is observable to the team and the
  board.
- **External engagement.** The CAO is one of
  several Halverston voices on AI, not the
  only one.
- **Honest self-assessment.** Including the
  honest naming of the CAO's own stalling
  symptoms (per Exercise 02 self-assessment).

The harder discipline: actively making the
function not depend on the current CAO. Two
specific things in year 3: the CAO will *not*
attend the AI Review Board for one quarter
(deputy chairs alone); the CAO will hand off
one regulator relationship to the deputy.

## 4. Reasoning notes

- **Why the consultant-for-vendor-AI-risk
  mitigation.** §6.3 — capability gaps can have
  non-hiring mitigations. The vendor AI risk
  specialist role is genuinely hard to fill;
  consultant engagement maintains coverage
  while the hire happens. Cost is real but
  acceptable.
- **Why the immediate succession is to the AI
  Risk Lead.** The AI Risk Lead has 2 years in
  role; understands the program; is respected
  by peers and the team; is a credible interim
  CAO. External interim is more expensive and
  loses continuity.
- **Why I named the AI Risk Lead as primary
  internal succession candidate.** The
  succession track is the same as the immediate
  succession path. The development plan in year
  3 is calibrated to the succession track.
- **Why the CAO's "not attend AI Review Board
  for a quarter" commitment is significant.**
  It's a forcing function. If the AI Review
  Board operates well without the CAO for a
  quarter, the function is genuinely delegated;
  if it doesn't, the delegation is incomplete.
  Either result is informative.
- **What this strategy under-specifies.**
  Specific compensation numbers; specific
  external candidate names; specific consultant
  engagement details. Those belong in HR / CRO
  documents.
