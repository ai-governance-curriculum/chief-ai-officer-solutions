# Module 112 — Quiz Answer Key

Answers and explanations for the [Module 112 quiz](https://github.com/ai-infra-curriculum/ai-infra-chief-ai-officer-learning/blob/main/lessons/mod-112-cao-operating-model/quiz.md).

---

## Section A — Operating model (§1)

**Q1. d. The CAO's personal preference** is not
in §1.2's list. The factors are organisation
size + complexity, regulatory landscape, existing
risk-function architecture, AI deployment
maturity.

**Q2. False.** §1.4 — changing the operating
model is expensive; year 1 or 2 is rarely the
right move regardless of how obvious the case
seems.

**Q3.** Any answer naming the operating model
chosen to optimise for the boundaries the CAO
function will spend the most time operating.

---

## Section B — Year 1 (§2)

**Q4. c. Author every program standard** is **not**
what year 1 should do. Year 1 produces
load-bearing standards; defers others.

**Q5. b.** The point at which foundation work
feels invisible and pressure mounts for visible
deliverables. §2.3.

**Q6.** Any §2.2 answer. Example: "year 1
should not try to solve every gap, build
comprehensive automation, or author every
standard at once — the discipline is choosing
what to address and what to defer."

---

## Section C — Year 2 (§3)

**Q7. b.** Consolidating and operating. §3.1.

**Q8. b.** Trying to fix every year-1 error
comprehensively. §3.3.

**Q9. False.** §3.4 — programs facing their
first material incident in year 2 are well-
positioned to respond.

**Q10.** Any §3.5 answer. Example: "the
year-2 self-assessment should show mature
controls at NIST Level 3, closed year-1 gaps,
substantive incident response track record, and
cross-LOB or cross-function integration."

---

## Section D — Year 3 (§4)

**Q11. d. Maximise headcount** is not in §4.1.
The five are operate efficiently, scale
practice, industry voice, leadership
succession, strategic ambition.

**Q12. b. Operational decisions.** §4.2 — the
shift is from operational to strategic /
relationship / external work.

**Q13.** Any §4.3 sign. Example: "CAO still
personally handling many operational decisions"
or "new AI systems requiring months to enter the
program's discipline" or "still building
foundational artifacts that should have been
complete in year 2".

---

## Section E — Calendar (§5)

**Q14. c. Monthly.** §5.1.

**Q15. d. Personal email** is not in §5.2's
protected categories. The four are strategic
thinking, direct report development,
stakeholder relationship-building, external
engagement.

**Q16. b. The AI Risk Lead.** §5.3.

**Q17.** Any §5.4 answer. Example: "the crisis
discipline acknowledges that incidents pull the
CAO into operations but disciplines getting back
to the recurring rhythm afterward with a
calendar-reset half-day within 2 weeks."

---

## Section F — Talent, budget, succession (§6)

**Q18. b.** Technical depth + risk discipline +
governance literacy + communication clarity.
§6.1.

**Q19. False.** §6.4 — the discipline is to be
*replaceable*, not irreplaceable.

**Q20.** Any §6.5 practice. Example: "peer CAO
network — monthly conversation with peers at
non-competing institutions — because the role is
isolating and the peer group is small;
external perspective is hard to come by
otherwise."
