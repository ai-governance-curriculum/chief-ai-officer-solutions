# Exercise 03 — Build a CAO Calendar — Reference Solution

## 1. Solution overview

A deliberate operating calendar covering all six
§5.1 cadences. Three protected time blocks
(Friday-afternoon strategic, weekly 1:1s, rolling
6-week external). One inviolable block (Friday
strategic thinking; only catastrophic incidents
interrupt). Crisis discipline with a 14-day
calendar-reset pattern.

## 3. Artifact 2 — Time protection

### Block 1 — Friday afternoon strategic thinking (INVIOLABLE)

- **When:** Friday 14:00-17:30 every week.
- **Discipline:** This block is inviolable. The
  only conditions that interrupt: a Category 1
  security incident; a Category 2/3 incident
  with material customer impact; a board chair's
  direct request.
- **Recovery pattern after disruption:** Reschedule
  to Saturday morning or to Monday early morning;
  do not let the disruption become permanent.

### Block 2 — Weekly direct-report 1:1s

- **When:** Tuesday-Thursday 30 minutes per
  direct report.
- **Discipline:** Treated as inviolable except
  for material incident response or board
  meetings.
- **Recovery pattern:** If missed, scheduled
  within 5 business days; not cancelled.

### Block 3 — Stakeholder relationship-building

- **When:** Monthly second-Monday 1:1s with CRO,
  CCO, CISO, MRM Lead; Friday business-unit-head
  rotation.
- **Discipline:** Scheduled monthly; not
  conditional on incident pressure.
- **Recovery pattern:** If a meeting is moved,
  reschedule within 14 days.

### Block 4 — External engagement

- **When:** Rolling 6-week external engagement
  calendar — regulator meeting; industry-body
  meeting; peer CAO call; conference or speaking
  engagement.
- **Discipline:** Approximately 1 external meeting
  per week on average. Not all are high-stakes;
  the discipline is the cadence, not the
  individual meeting.
- **Recovery pattern:** Skip-and-reschedule one
  week at most; further deferral signals
  systematic over-prioritisation of internal
  work.

### Crisis discipline statement

When a material incident occurs:

- **Days 1-3 of incident:** All non-critical
  calendar items cleared. CAO operates on
  incident-response calendar.
- **Days 4-7:** Critical recurring items
  (CRO 1:1; AI Risk Council if scheduled)
  preserved; others cleared.
- **Days 8-14:** Recurring items restart;
  incident response continues but on schedule.

**Reset pattern:** Within 2 weeks of incident
closure, schedule a 4-hour "calendar reset"
block. The block is used to: re-confirm all
protected time blocks; reschedule any missed
1:1s; assess whether any items should be
restructured given lessons learned.

The reset pattern matters because the calendar
drifts toward reactive operation after each
crisis; without deliberate reset, the
recurring rhythm degrades incident-by-incident.

---

## 4. Reasoning notes

- **Why Friday afternoon for strategic thinking.**
  Most internal meeting demand is Monday-
  Thursday; Friday afternoon is the natural
  protected block. Some organisations have
  Monday morning as the alternative;
  practitioners differ.
- **Why 1:1s are treated as inviolable.** Direct
  report development is the highest-leverage
  thing the CAO does for the program's medium-
  term capability. Cancelling 1:1s for incidents
  is the failure mode that produces
  under-developed deputies.
- **Why I delegated several items.** §5.3 — the
  daily standup, weekly bias summary, and
  AI Review Board are AI Risk Lead's work.
  The CAO observing or being consulted is
  different from the CAO being owner.
- **Why I named the calendar-reset pattern
  specifically.** §5.4 — crises pull the
  calendar toward reactive operation. Without a
  deliberate reset, the recurring rhythm
  degrades over time. The 4-hour block is the
  forcing function.
- **What this calendar deliberately doesn't
  include.** Email/Slack time; reading and
  research time (not block-scheduled; happens
  during travel and dispersed); personal
  development beyond the named external
  engagement.
